3.9 
Post-processing heuris6c 
The last step of the modelling pipeline is the post-processing heuris=c (PPH). Compared to using a sta=c 
probability threshold, it can be considered as a more elaborate technique to convert the lane slot connec=on 
probabili=es into binary outcomes. This is done through a set of rules to avoid inconsistent turn predic=ons. For 
example, if a par=cular lane slot combina=on has been found to be connected, then there can be no lane slot 
connec=ons with an exit lane slot to the leb of the approach lane of the combina=on that was found to be true. 
These movements are then ﬂagged as inconsistent. The heuris=c starts with the highest probability in the 
adjacency matrix, ﬂags it as a consistent turn, and progressively applies the heuris=c rules in descending order of 
probability un=l all turns are ﬂagged as either consistent or inconsistent. A new probability threshold to yield 
binarised predic=ons can subsequently be determined considering only the consistent turns. Form each arm, the 
heuris=c rules applied are: 
• No exit lanes to the leb of an approach lane 
• No approach lanes to the right of an exit lane 
• No through or right turns to the leb of a leb turn 
• No leb turns to the right of a through or right turn 
• No leb or though turn to the right of a right turn 
• No turns from the same approach arm to the same exit arm and lane slot
